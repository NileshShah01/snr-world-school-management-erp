Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your data

1. Introduction

Apex Public School ("we", "our", "the School") is committed to protecting the privacy of students, parents, and staff. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and safeguard personal data when you use our platform, website, and related services.

We comply with the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 of India and applicable data protection laws.

Last Updated: June 2026

2. Data We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Student Information: Full name, date of birth, class/section, roll number, Aadhar number (optional), photographs, academic records, attendance, and examination results.
  • Parent/Guardian Information: Full name, relationship to student, phone number, email address, and residential address.
  • Staff Information: Name, contact details, qualification, employment history, and payroll data.
  • Usage Data: Login timestamps, IP addresses, browser type, and feature interaction logs for security and improvement.

3. How We Use Your Data

Your data is used exclusively for educational and administrative purposes:

  • Academic tracking and report card generation
  • Attendance monitoring and communication with parents
  • Fee management and payment processing
  • Examination scheduling and result publishing
  • School-wide announcements and notifications
  • Statutory reporting to educational authorities
  • Platform improvement and security monitoring

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data to third parties. Data is shared only with:

  • The School — authorized staff and administrators
  • Statutory Bodies — as required by law (e.g., Department of Education)
  • Service Providers — Google (Firebase infrastructure, India region), payment gateways (for fee processing only)

All service providers are contractually bound to maintain confidentiality and comply with applicable data protection laws.

5. Data Storage & Security

Data is stored on Google Cloud (Firebase) servers located in the India region. We implement industry-standard security measures including:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Multi-factor authentication for administrative accounts
  • Role-based access controls (RBAC)
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Firestore security rules enforcing least-privilege access

6. Your Rights

Under DPDP Act 2023, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access — request a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Correction — update inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Deletion — request erasure of your data (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Right to Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to Grievance Redressal — lodge a complaint with the Data Protection Officer

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer (see Section 9).

7. Children's Data

We collect and process data of children (under 18 years) only with verifiable parental consent. Parents or legal guardians have the right to:

  • Review their child's data at any time
  • Withdraw consent and request deletion
  • Restrict processing to specific purposes

Consent is obtained at the time of admission via the consent checkbox on the admission form. Withdrawal of consent may affect the School's ability to provide certain services.

8. Cookies & Tracking

Our platform uses no cookies or tracking technologies for advertising or analytics. Firebase authentication uses local storage tokens for session management only. No third-party tracking scripts are employed.

10. Data Retention Policy

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary:

  • Active student records: Duration of enrollment + 7 years
  • Graduated alumni records: Archived after 7 years
  • Financial records: 8 years (as per Indian tax law)
  • Inquiry records: 1 year
  • Audit logs: 3 years

After the retention period, data is soft-deleted and permanently purged within 90 days.
